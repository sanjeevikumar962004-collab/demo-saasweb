The story so far

Built from frustration. Grown through obsession.

Every company has a founding myth. Ours involves a broken Zapier workflow, a very long Slack thread, and a bet that there had to be a better way.

2021 — The spark

Two engineers, one big problem

Daniel Park and Aisha Nkemdi were ops engineers at a Series B startup when they spent a combined 80 hours in a single month manually fixing broken data syncs between Salesforce and their data warehouse. They quit on the same Friday and started Stackly the following Monday.

2022 — First traction

10 customers. $0 in marketing.

The first version was a Notion doc with embedded API calls. It was ugly, barely worked, and had zero onboarding. Somehow, ten companies paid for it anyway. Those first customers shaped every feature we've built since — and most of them are still with us today.

2023 — The AI leap

When GPT-4 changed everything

We rewrote the core routing engine overnight when GPT-4 was released. Context-aware routing replaced 2,000 brittle rules for one of our enterprise customers. The results were so dramatic we pivoted the entire product roadmap in six weeks. Best decision we ever made.

2024–Present

$24M Series B. 12,000 customers. Just getting started.

With backing from Sequoia and a16z, we've scaled to 48 people across San Francisco, London, and Singapore. We process 98 million workflow executions per month and we're still obsessing over the same thing we were on day one: making automation feel effortless.

What we believe

Six principles we never compromise on.

01

Speed over perfection

Shipping a good feature fast beats perfecting a feature slowly. We move at startup speed because our customers need leverage today, not next quarter.

02

Radical transparency

Our status page is always public. Our roadmap is always shared. We tell customers bad news first. Trust is built in minutes and lost in seconds.

03

Customer obsession

Every engineer talks to customers every week. Product decisions live or die by what real users actually need — not what we think they need.

04

Simplicity is the product

Every feature we add is a feature a customer has to learn. We'd rather make one thing work brilliantly than ship ten things that are merely fine.

05

Own the outcome

We don't blame infrastructure, third-party APIs, or edge cases. If something breaks for a customer, it's our problem to fix — no excuses, no tickets closed without resolution.

06

Async by default

We write things down. Decisions live in documents, not memories. Meetings are a last resort, not a first instinct. Deep work is non-negotiable.
